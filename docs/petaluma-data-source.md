### Global Measure of Wellbeing (NOT in standard Raworth 12)
- **Source:** Page 13, *The Amsterdam City Doughnut*
- **Petaluma context note:** Petaluma participates in a global political economy through the goods and services its residents consume. Many products are imported, and their production carries social and ecological impacts beyond city boundaries (labor conditions, resource extraction, ecological degradation).
- **Methodology note:** No single indicator can capture this fully — supply chains span countries and regulatory regimes.
- **Leverage:** Procurement choices, consumer demand, institutional purchasing, and local policy can influence upstream conditions. A deeper assessment could map Petaluma's import patterns and supply-chain dependencies.
- **John's suggestion:** Petaluma receives a 1% tax on all Amazon purchases — could work with the city's new Economic Development person.
- **Peggy's note:** Want to go after consumerism; buying local is in the action plans. Volume of farmers markets could be a good indicator.
- **Schema decision needed:** This isn't a single Raworth dimension — it's the DEAL **"global-social" lens** from the four-lens City Portrait method. Could be presented as a separate panel/page rather than a 13th dimension. **Amelia to decide.**

## Ecological Ceiling Indicators

### Climate Change
- **Indicator:** Per capita GHG emissions (DEAL "safe and just space" target ≈ 1.6–2.0 tons CO2e/person, aligned with 1.5 °C Paris goal)
- **Data point:** **7.25 metric tons CO2e / person** (Petaluma community-wide emissions ≈ 435,000 MTCO2e ÷ ~60,000 residents)
- **Source:** [Petaluma Blueprint for Climate Action](https://www.planpetaluma.org/blueprint-for-climate-action)

### Air Pollution
- **Indicator:** PM2.5 annual average
- **Data point:** Typically **7.0–9.5 μg/m³**
- **Source:** [BAAQMD Air Monitoring](https://www.baaqmd.gov/en/about-air-quality/current-air-quality/air-monitoring-data/#/aqi-highs?date=2026-04-08&view=hourly)

### Land Use & Biodiversity
- **Indicator:** % protected land / sea area
- **Data point:** Within the Petaluma Valley / Sonoma Mountain area, ~**22%** of land is protected under conservation easements or public ownership.
- **Source:** [Sonoma Mountain — Protected Open Space](https://sonomamountain.org/preservation/protected-open-space/)
- **Schema note:** This single Petaluma category maps to **two** standard Raworth dimensions: *land conversion* and *biodiversity loss*. Same data point used for both as a first pass; biodiversity-specific data (e.g., endemic species, habitat condition) still to come.

### Nutrient Loading
- **Indicator:** Nitrogen leached to groundwater per year
- **Data point:** Most monitoring wells show levels below the 10 mg/L limit; hotspots near agricultural boundaries often show **4–7 mg/L**, indicating moderate loading from fertilizers and septic systems.
- **Source:** [Petaluma Valley Groundwater WY2024 Annual Report](https://petalumavalleygroundwater.org/wp-content/uploads/PV-Annual-Report_WY2024-ADA.pdf)

### Chemical Pollution
- **Indicator:** Pesticide use
- **Data point:** Need more input from City.

### Waste & Materials (NOT in standard Raworth 9)
- **Indicator:** % of waste sent to landfill or overseas
- **Data point:** Need more input from City.
- **Schema decision needed:** Not a standard planetary boundary. Treat as supplementary local indicator or fold into chemical-pollution / land-conversion narratives. **Amelia to decide.**

### Global Consumption-Based Footprint (NOT in standard Raworth 9)
- See note on Global Measure of Wellbeing (social side). Same schema decision applies — this maps to DEAL's **"global-ecological" lens**, not a single boundary.
